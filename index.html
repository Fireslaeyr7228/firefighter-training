Why rest matters at 47
CNS recovery slows with age. Rushing rest on heavy sets means your nervous system hasn't fully recovered — you'll lift less, form breaks down, and injury risk goes up. The 3–5 minute window on big lifts isn't optional padding; it's what separates training from grinding. Use the time to breathe, reset your brace, and visualize the next set.
12-week plan

Sessions 1–8 — Orientation

Learn the movements, stay at RPE 6. Focus on form especially hip hinge and front squat.

Sessions 9–24 — Build volume

When all sets feel comfortable, add 5 lb upper / 10 lb lower. Track every session. RPE 7 target.

Every ~7th session — Deload

Cut volume 50%, keep intensity. Do this by session count, not calendar week.

Sessions 26–44 — Intensity phase

Reduce volume slightly, increase load. Work toward 3–4RM on main lifts. RPE 8–9.

Session 45+ — Retest

Trap bar deadlift 5RM · Front squat 5RM · Bench press 5RM · Max pull-ups · 2km row time

Shift week rule

Coming off a hard 24-hour shift counts as a training day. Treat the next session as a deload regardless of where you are in the program.
